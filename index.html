Here, I have a snippet of a town's theme in the iconic Pokemon Red game. Known as the Lavender Town theme, this song is fueled with spooky and unsettling binuaral beeps. Due to the eeriness of the song, it's led to rumorous folktales surrounding the game. In certain instances some people believed the fanmade rumour of children commiting suicide due to the beats of the song. While these rumours were put to rest and proven false, this concept of sound in game shows the power that sound can have over the player. What I find fascinating about the impact that the audio has on the human correlates directly to Didier Anzieu's, The Skin Ego. Reading the work and understadning the impact sound can have on a baby shows how even as we leave young stages we're still impacted by Anzieu's thought on the sound mirror. When I think of how this sound mirror is disrupted, it's in part because the audio of this track is unfamilar and unknowing. The audio purposefully, as intended, sends the player into the mindset of uncomfortabilty, panic and anxiety.
